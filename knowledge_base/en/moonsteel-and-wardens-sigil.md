# Moonsteel and the Warden's Sigil

Two things mark an **ashwarden** above all: a blade of **moonsteel** and the **warden's sigil** worn at the throat.

**Moonsteel** is a rare silvery alloy, smelted with old rites in the forges of **Vael Korhal**, that bites into creatures of the **Aether** as ordinary steel never can. Most monsters of **Aerdmir** — a **groveshade**, a **curseborn**, a **skyrender** — shrug off common weapons but burn at the touch of moonsteel. For this reason an ashwarden carries two blades: moonsteel for monsters, plain steel for men. Moonsteel is costly and difficult to work, and a warden guards their blade as they would their own life.

The **warden's sigil** is a small medallion cast in the shape of each lodge's emblem — a lynx, for the **Lodge of the Lynx**. More than a badge, the sigil trembles or grows cold in the presence of strong **Aether** or a nearby monster, giving the warden a vital early warning. It also marks its bearer to those who know the old orders, drawing equal measures of fear, respect, and hatred.

Together, blade and sigil are the working tools of the trade — as essential to **Korven of Drael** and **Varmun** as their **Glyphs** and their **elixirs**.
