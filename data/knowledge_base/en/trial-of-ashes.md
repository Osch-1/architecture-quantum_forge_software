# The Trial of Ashes

The **Trial of Ashes** is the brutal rite that transforms an ordinary child into an **ashwarden**. Conducted in secret within strongholds like **Vael Korhal**, it subjects initiates to a punishing regimen of alchemical **reagents** that rewrite the body — granting heightened senses, reflexes, strength, and the resistance to poison that lets a warden drink lethal **elixirs**, but at a terrible cost.

Most children do not survive the Trial. Those who do emerge changed in body and appearance — the pale hair and strange eyes of **Korven of Drael** are its mark — and sterile, unable to pass on their line, so that the ashwardens must always recruit anew. The survivors gain the capacity to channel **Aether** through the **Glyphs**, the first step on the long road of the hunter.

The Trial is the central reason ashwardens are both needed and feared: the same process that makes them able to face a **groveshade** or a **curseborn** also marks them as unnatural in the eyes of common folk. As lodges like the **Lodge of the Lynx** dwindle, fewer and fewer endure the Trial of Ashes, and the secret of the rite itself risks being lost. Old **Olvar** is among the last who still knows how to perform it.
